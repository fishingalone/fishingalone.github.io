Interpolation Spaces

Background

Let's say there are two normed spaces \( X_0\) and \( X_1\). We call \( X_0\) and \( X_1\) a compatible pair \( X=(X_0,X_1)\) if they are subspaces of some larger vector space. The intersection of the two spaces is a normed space defined with \[ \| u\|_{X_0\cap X_1} := \left( \| u\|_{X_0}^2 + \| u\|_{X_1}^2 \right)^{1/2}. \] The sum of the two spaces is a normed space with \[ \| u\|_{X_0+X_1} := \inf \left\{ \| u_0\|_{X_0}^2 + \| u_1\|_{X_1}^2 \, : \, u=u_0+u_1\text{ where } u_0\in X_0, u_1\in X_1\right\}^{1/2}. \] Notice that the inclusions \[ X_0 \cap X_1 \subset X_j \subset X_0+X_1, \quad j=0,1, \] are continuous because \[ \| u\|_{X_0+X_1} \leq \| u\|_{X_j} \leq \| u\|_{X_0\cap X_1}. \] Given a compatible pair, our objective is to define a family of normed spaces \[ X_{\theta, q} = (X_0,X_1)_{\theta,q},\quad \text{for } 0<\theta <1 \text{ and } 1\leq q \leq \infty. \] For technical reasons, we will construct \( X_{\theta, q}\) in two ways. Thus we shall define two spaces \( K_{\theta,q} (X)\) and \( J_{theta,q} (X)\) and show \[ X_{\theta, q} = K_{\theta,q} (X) = J_{\theta,q} (X) \] with equivalent norms. The K-method will be used to prove the interpolation properties of \( H^s(\Omega )\), after which the interpolation properties of \( \wildetilde{H}^s (\Omega)\) follow by a duality argument that relies on the J-method.
