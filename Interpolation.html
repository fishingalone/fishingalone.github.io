Interpolation Spaces

Background

Let's say there are two normed spaces \( X_0\) and \( X_1\). We call \( X_0\) and \( X_1\) a compatible pair \( X=(X_0,X_1)\) if they are subspaces of some larger vector space. The intersection of the two spaces is a normed space defined with \[ \| u\|_{X_0\cap X_1} := \left( \| u\|_{X_0}^2 + \| u\|_{X_1}^2 \right)^{1/2}. \] The sum of the two spaces is a normed space with \[ \| u\|_{X_0+X_1} := \inf_{\substack{u_0\in X_0, u_1\in X_1\\\text{s.t. } u=u_0+u_1}} \left\{ \left( \| u_0\|_{X_0}^2 + \| u_1\|_{X_1}^2\right)^{1/2} \right\}. \] Notice that the inclusions \[ X_0 \cap X_1 \subset X_j \subset X_0+X_1, \quad j=0,1, \] are continuous because \[ \| u\|_{X_0+X_1} \leq \| u\|_{X_j} \leq \| u\|_{X_0\cap X_1}. \] Given a compatible pair, our objective is to construct a family of normed spaces \[ X_{\theta, q} = (X_0,X_1)_{\theta,q},\quad \text{for } 0<\theta <1 \text{ and } 1\leq q \leq \infty. \] For technical reasons, we will construct \( X_{\theta, q}\) in two ways. Thus we shall define two spaces \( K_{\theta,q} (X)\) and \( J_{\theta,q} (X)\) and show \[ X_{\theta, q} = K_{\theta,q} (X) = J_{\theta,q} (X) \] with equivalent norms. The K-method will be used to prove the interpolation properties of \( H^s(\Omega )\), after which the interpolation properties of \( \widetilde{H}^s (\Omega)\) follow by a duality argument that relies on the J-method.

The K-Method

The K-functional is defined for \( t>0\) and \( u\in X_0+X_1\) by \begin{equation} \label{eq:1} K(t,u) := \inf_{\substack{u_0\in X_0, u_1\in X_1\\\text{s.t. } u=u_0+u_1}} \left\{ \left( \| u_0\|_{X_0}^2 + t^2\| u_1\|_{X_1}^2\right)^{1/2} \right\}. \end{equation} Next, we define a weighted \( L_q\)-norm, \[ \| f\|_{\theta,q} := \left( \int_0^\infty |t^{-\theta} f(t)|^q {\mathrm{d}t\over t}\right)^{1/q},\quad \text{ for } 0<\theta <1\text{ and } 1\leq q<\infty, \] with the obvious modification \[ \| f\|_{\theta,\infty} := \mathop{\mathrm{ess\,sup}}_{t>0} | t^{-\theta} f(t) |. \] Now define the normed space \[ K_{\theta,q} (X) := \left\{ u\in X_0+X_1 \, :\, \| K(\cdot, u)\|_{\theta, q} <\infty\right\} \] with the weighted \( L_q\)-norm of the K-functional, \[ \| u\|_{K_{\theta,q}(X)} := \| K(\cdot, u)\|_{\theta,q}. \] We are able to prove the following:
Lemma 1 If \( u\in X_0\cap X_1\), then \( u\in K_{\theta,q}(X)\) and there exists a constant \( C\) only depending on \( q\) and \( \theta\) such that \[ \| u\|_{K_{\theta,q}(X)} \leq C \| u\|_{X_0}^{1-\theta}\| u\|_{X_1}^\theta. \]

By definition \eqref{eq:1}, \[ K(t,u) \leq \min (\| u\|_{X_0}, t\| u\|_{X_1} ) = \| u\|_{X_0} \min (1, at), \] where we let \( a := \| u\|_{X_1}/\| u\|_{X_0}\). The special case \( u=0\) is trivial. Then we deduce from the scaling and dilatation property (\( \| t\mapsto f(at)\|_{\theta,q} = a^\theta \| f\|_{\theta,q}\) ) of the weighted norm that \[ \| K(\cdot, u)\|_{\theta,q} = \| u\|_{X_0} a^\theta \| \min (1,\cdot)\|_{\theta,q} = C \| u\|_{X_0}^{1-\theta} \| u\|_{X_1}^\theta. \]

Proposition 1 The inclusion \( X_0\cap X_1\subset K_{\theta,q}(X)\) is continuous, or equivalently, there exists a constant \( C\) only depending on \( q\) and \( \theta\) such that \[ \| u\|_{K_{\theta,q}(X)}\leq C \| u\|_{X_0\cap X_1}. \]

By Young's inequality with \( p = (1-\theta)^{-1}\) and \( p^* = 1/(1-1/p) = \theta^{-1}\), \[ \| u\|_{X_0}^{1-\theta} \| u\|_{X_1}^\theta \leq {\left( \| u\|_{X_0}^{1-\theta}\right)^p\over p} + {\left( \| u\|_{X_1}^\theta \right)^{p^*}\over p^*} = (1-\theta)\| u\|_{X_0} + \theta \| u\|_{X_1} \leq \left[ (1-\theta)^2+\theta^2 \right]^{1/2} \| u\|_{X_0\cap X_1}. \] The result then follows from Lemma 1.

Proposition 2 The inclusion \( K_{\theta,q}(X)\subset X_0+X_1\) is continuous, or equivalently, there exists a constant \( C\) only depending on \( q\) and \( \theta\) such that \[ \| u\|_{X_0+X_1}\leq C\| u\|_{K_{\theta,q}(X)}. \]

It obviously follows from taking the weighted norm on the both sides of \[ \min (1,t) \| u\|_{X_0+X_1} \leq K(t,u). \]
